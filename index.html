02 — Fundamentals

The protocol behind the ticker

Grove is the Sky Ecosystem's institutional credit allocation layer — routing USDS liquidity into AAA-rated CLO tranches, private credit, and tokenized T-bills through non-custodial vaults. Incubated by Grove Labs (Steakhouse Financial). The protocol launched in June 2025 with a $1B Sky allocation into the Janus Henderson Anemoy AAA CLO strategy.

How GROVE reaches holdersiSources: Grove blog — Claim your GROVE (Jul 6, 2026) · Sky Forum — Grove Farm scope · @SkyEcosystem

Eligibility claim — an early-community allocation, claimable only at app.grove.finance (Claim card → connect wallet → check eligibility). Claims run on Ethereum with a 90-day window; unclaimed allocations are forfeited and can't be recovered.

Sky Ecosystem Rewards — the primary ongoing route: supply USDS on Sky.money and accrue GROVE, with parameters and schedule set by Sky governance.

USDS → GROVE farm — a StakingRewards farm fed by a treasury vest that streams pre-allocated GROVE; deposits stay withdrawable by the user.

Open market — Coinbase spot (GROVE-USD; ~87% of volume on listing day, Jul 6) and Uniswap v4 pools on Ethereum. The split moves as venues change — the live share is in the Analytics tab.

app.grove.finance is the only official claim link. Grove warns against any third-party links, forms, or contracts claiming to distribute GROVE — official information comes only from grove.finance, Grove Docs, and verified Grove and Sky channels.
03 — Farm record

Farm mechanics & activity

How the GROVE farm worksiSources: Sky executive vote (Jul 2, 2026) · Sky.money — Ecosystem Rewards · Sky Forum — Grove Farm technical scope

Live now — GROVE is distributed through Sky Ecosystem Rewards on Sky.money: supply USDS and accrue GROVE in proportion to your share of the pool. A set amount of GROVE is distributed, so the effective rate floats with pool size. No lockups, no minimum holding period, no exit fees — the underlying USDS stays withdrawable.

The stream — the Jul 2, 2026 executive spell (earliest execution timed to listing day, Jul 6 at 18:00 UTC) initializes the farm with a treasury vest streaming 2.45B GROVE over 730 days — 24.5% of total supply, ≈3.36M GROVE per day at the base rate — with distributions pushed to the farm roughly weekly by a keeper job.

Under the hood — a StakingRewards contract where users stake USDS, fed by a DssVestTransferrable treasury vest through a VestedRewardsDistribution contract. Same architecture as Spark's SPK farm; only the USDS → GROVE farm exists at launch (no staked-SKY farm).

04 — Sources & method

Where every number comes from

Live figures

Two sources, environment-dependent. Viewed inside Claude, the artifact sandbox blocks third-party APIs, so figures arrive via Claude's live web search — which is directed to read CoinGecko's Grove listing (ID grove-2, verified against the official contract 0xB30F…9406). Hosted or opened outside Claude, the page pulls the same figures directly from CoinGecko's public API. The status bar always names the source that produced the numbers on screen. Charts and the venue split use CoinGecko's market-history and tickers endpoints. Farm figures are read on-chain — totalSupply, rewardRate, and periodFinish straight from the REWARDS_USDS_GROVE farm contract — and the transfer feed reads Transfer events from the GROVE token contract via public Ethereum RPCs, falling back to Blockscout. Farm history is reconstructed client-side: totalSupply and rewardRate are eth_call\u2019d at ~evenly sampled historical blocks over archival public RPCs, joined with the CoinGecko price series for APR — sampling holds ~100 points regardless of farm age. Supply basis: circulating supply and market cap are displayed live from CoinGecko and never hardcoded — during launch week (Jul 7) CoinGecko revised GROVE’s circulating basis, so any static claim about it goes stale; the page also flags if price × circulating stops matching the reported market cap.

Guardrails on the web-search layer. The query is scoped to the contract, look-alike tickers are excluded by name — GroveCoin (GRV), the GroveWars token on Theta, and GROOVE all pollute aggregators — and it's instructed to leave a blank rather than guess. Sources found on each refresh appear as linked chips in the Market pulse card. This layer requires the viewer to be signed in to Claude; the direct CoinGecko and on-chain paths do not.

Live figures can lag real-time and should be verified against an exchange or Grove Data before external use.
